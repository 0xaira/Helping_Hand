Asset Funding and Financial Assistance.

Finance is the lifeblood of any business, be it small or big. One of the biggest challenges women entrepreneurs face in India is the lack of finance. Govertment Schmes helps them to deal with this by :

  • Offering financial support to women entrepreneurs in agriculture, manufacturing, micro-credit, retail stores, or small enterprises.
  • Motivating women's entrepreneurship among the poor majorly supporting and helping illiterate women living in rural and backward areas.
  • Providing opportunities of self-employment to the educated young women by giving grants to commence their personal business project and subsequently create scope of employment for others.
2

Skill Training and Leadership

Women-owned companies are still in the minority and face more hurdles as compared to their male counterparts. Govertment Schmes helps them to deal with this by :

  • Providing skill training to women in the country which aims at stimulating employment opportunities among women of various socio-economic levels and different age groups.
  • Giving employability to women and to provide competencies and skill that enable women to become self-employed/entrepreneurs.
  • Improving the occupational skills and technical knowledge of the non/neo literates and persons having rudimentary level of education
3

Education, Counselling & Guidance

The female literacy rate in India is around 65.46% as compared to the 74% male literacy rate. Illiteracy has always been the root cause of several socioeconomic problems in India. Govertment Schmes helps them to deal with this by :

  • Addressing declining CSR, propagating education, protection, and the survival of girls.
  • Improving quality of education imparted at secondary level, removal of gender, socio-economic and disability barriers.
  • Empowering rural women by educating them about digital literacy, employment opportunities, skill development, health, and nutrition.
